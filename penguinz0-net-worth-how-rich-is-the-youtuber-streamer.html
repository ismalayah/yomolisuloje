Penguinz0 Net Worth Early Life

Charles White was born in Tampa, Florida on August 2, 1994. He held religious views when he was younger, but no longer does. He went to Carrollwood Day School and played on the varsity basketball team. White suffered from recurrent pneumothorax in his left lung, which collapsed three times before undergoing surgery in 2011.

During his adolescence, White suffered from obsessive–compulsive disorder and engaged in “strange routines.” He earned a bachelor’s degree in human sciences from The University of Tampa.

Penguinz0 Net Worth & Streaming Career

White began creating YouTube videos in 2006, when he began uploading music and AMVs to his previous channels. On May 7, 2007, he launched his penguinz0 YouTube account. On August 5, he posted his first non-deleted video, which included Gears of War gameplay. From late 2009 until early 2010, he was streaming on Justin.tv.

White’s following rose fast after he posted a video titled The Most Difficult Game Ever Created Gameplay and Commentary, in which he comments while playing the online game QWOP and refers to it as “the hardest game he has ever played.” The video went viral after Ray William Johnson included it on his Equals Three show, where he praised White’s sense of comedy. Tubefilter reported White in 2013 as criticizing YouTube’s new comment system, which needed a Google+ account to make comments on videos.

White stated in 2012 that he wished to donate all of his YouTube profits to charity. Later that year, he announced a relationship with Maker Studios and the creation of a Twitter account to publicize his charitable contributions. On September 2, 2014, he posted a video thanking his fans for helping him hit the million-subscriber mark, and he stated that he had contributed over $50,000 from his YouTube profits to charity.

He co-hosted an online Super Smash Bros. Ultimate tournament on his Twitch channel with fellow YouTuber Alpharad in March 2020, owing to COVID-19 lockdown restrictions.

White’s fame rose in late 2020 as a result of his Twitch streams about chess and Among Us with prominent streamers like Sykkuno, Pokimane, Nigahiga, Valkyrae, Trainwreckstv, and Disguised Toast. White took part in the first PogChamps chess competition, which was sponsored by Chess.com in June. He won “one of the tournament’s more anticipated games,” defeating fellow Twitch player xQc in six moves. According to Dot Esports, the game was one of the five “greatest events in streaming from 2020,” and the video of White’s triumph became one of the most-watched in Twitch ever, with over 2 million views by December 24, 2020. White took first place in the tournament’s consolation bracket.

White inked a contract with BroadbandTV Corp (BBTV) in November to become a content partner with the firm. Also in November, White discussed an uptick in Twitch’s DMCA crackdowns in a YouTube video. In December, White took part in Twitch’s “Pokémon Week,” which included many producers holding streams in which they unwrapped boxes of Pokémon cards.
